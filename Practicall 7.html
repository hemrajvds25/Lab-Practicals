Tribute to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

life of chhatrapati shivaji maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was a visionary warrior-king who founded the Maratha Empire in western India by fighting against the Bijapur Sultanate and the Mughal Empire. Born at Shivneri Fort to Shahaji and Jijabai, he pioneered guerrilla warfare (Ganimi Kava), established a strong navy, and was crowned king at Raigad in 1674.

Achievement of Chhatrapati

  • Early Life & Rise: Born on February 19, 1630, he was raised by his mother, Jijabai, who taught him about righteousness and Swaraj (self-rule). At only 16, he captured Torna Fort, marking his first victory.
  • Military Genius: He captured several strategic forts, including Kondana, Raigad, and Purandhar. He is famous for defeating Afzal Khan of Bijapur in 1659 using clever tactics.
  • Conflicts with Mughals: He famously escaped from Aurangzeb’s house arrest in Agra in 1666.
  • Administration & Values: He was a just and secular ruler, protecting the rights of women, farmers, and respecting all religions.
  • Coronation & Legacy: He was formally crowned as Chhatrapati (Paramount Sovereign) on June 6, 1674, at Raigad Fort. He died on April 3, 1680, leaving behind a legacy of bravery, patriotism, and efficient administration.
